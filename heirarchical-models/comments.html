centered vs. non-centerd

Many different ways to parameterize a model. Two common examples are to

centered models

[yij∣…]∼Normal(θj,σy2)[θj∣…]∼Normal(μ,σθ2) \begin{aligned} [y_{ij}|\ldots] &\sim \text{Normal}(\theta_j, \sigma_y^2) \\ [\theta_j|\ldots] &\sim \text{Normal}(\mu, \sigma_\theta^2) \end{aligned} [yij​∣…][θj​∣…]​∼Normal(θj​,σy2​)∼Normal(μ,σθ2​)​

non-centered

[yij∣…]∼Normal(μ+γj,σy2)[γj∣…]∼Normal(0,σθ2) \begin{aligned} [y_{ij}|\ldots] &\sim \text{Normal}(\mu + \gamma_j, \sigma_y^2) \\ [\gamma_j|\ldots] &\sim \text{Normal}(0, \sigma_\theta^2) \end{aligned} [yij​∣…][γj​∣…]​∼Normal(μ+γj​,σy2​)∼Normal(0,σθ2​)​
